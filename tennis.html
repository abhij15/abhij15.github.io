My Game

Playing Style

My game is built around consistency and court awareness. I'm a baseline player at heart — I prefer longer rallies where I can dictate pace and exploit angles. My forehand is my weapon: a heavy topspin shot that I can redirect cross-court or rip down the line when I'm on the attack.

Defensively, I've always taken pride in my footwork and ability to retrieve difficult balls. Growing up watching players like Rafael Nadal made me appreciate how far relentless defense and physical endurance can take you in a match. After returning to tennis recently, I've been focused on rebuilding consistency on my serve and sharpening my second-ball attack.

Beyond strokes, the mental component of tennis is what separates good players from great ones. Staying composed during momentum shifts, managing frustration after errors, and maintaining a competitive mindset through a long third set — these are things I work on just as deliberately as any technical skill.

Tennis has also shaped how I approach everything off the court. The discipline, focus, and resilience required to compete have directly carried over into how I handle academics, recruiting, and personal challenges. It's not just a sport — it's a mindset I bring everywhere.
