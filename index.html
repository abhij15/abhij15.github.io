About Me

Hi, I'm Abhiram, a sophomore at Northeastern University studying Data Science and Business Administration. But if you asked me what defines me outside the classroom, the answer is simple: tennis.

I've been playing tennis since I was a kid growing up in Simi Valley, California. What started as a recreational hobby quickly became a serious pursuit. By high school, I had worked my way up to become the varsity captain of my school's tennis team, a role that taught me far more than just how to hit a backhand. It taught me leadership, resilience, and how to perform under pressure.

Tennis is a sport of relentless precision. Every shot demands technical mastery including the angle of your racket, the weight transfer through your legs, and the snap of your wrist at contact. But beyond the technique, it's a deeply psychological game. You are out there alone on the court, no teammates to lean on, having to find solutions in real time.

After a period away from the game during college, I've recently returned to playing and have been reminded of just how much I missed it. Tennis isn't just a sport to me, it's a way of thinking, a way of competing, and a way of staying grounded. I follow the professional tour closely and draw constant inspiration from players like Carlos Alcaraz, whose explosive athleticism and mental toughness set a benchmark I aspire to.
